Published Articles

Reviewing Proposed FISA Reforms

Published for the American Action Forum, this article analyzes the 2023 reauthorization of FISA Title VII. Working with my colleague Josh Levine, we report on how the reauthorization of FISA Title VII hinges on implementing reforms to rein in the intelligence community's reported abuses.

The Lightning Fast Adoption of the North American Charging Standard

Published for the Policy Intern Blog, this article explains the implementation of the North American Charging Standard, a type of electric vehicle charging port pioneered by Tesla. As other automakers and states shift towards this preferred method, there is increased pressure for federal funding for charging to reflect this shift.

The State of United States and European Data Privacy Legislation

Coming soon for the American Action Forum, this piece examines the differing approaches to data privacy legislation. The United States has a patchwork of federal sectoral laws and multiple state regulatory schemes, creating an environment that could potentially lead to $1 trillion in business costs. Europe has an equally anticompetitive system, with GDPR effectively killing tech innovation in the EU. As the United States looks to implement a federal privacy law, considering the downstream effects of such legislation is essential.

The Severe Consequences to Increased Cloud Computing Regulation

Coming soon for the American Action Forum, this piece looks at the impact regulation could have on the cloud computing industry. In recent years, the cloud computing industry has begun to gain regulatory scrutiny, driven by the market penetration of the three main providers: Amazon, Microsoft, and Google. Destabilizing the ecosystem in the name of increased competition would have disastrous implications for customers and consumers.

Network Analysis of Relationships Between Candidates and Political Action Committees

Coming soon, this research will evaluate the interconnectedness of the American political system by following the money. Utilizing Python to create a digital network, this report will analyze the differences between incumbent and challenger funding, and examine the shocking similarities between Democrats and Republicans.

How is the United States Preparing for the Next Pandemic?

A report for Fundamentals of Health Policy and Management (PPD 325) examines the body of research regarding pandemic preparedness. Drawing on the successes of Korea, the PREVENT Act, and the Lancet's Commission's report, it looks at different perspectives on what the United States should do to prepare for the next pandemic.

Redistribution of LAPD Budget for Hate Crime Awareness

A group report for Social Justice Issues in Public Policy (PPD 300) examines the incidence of hate crimes against Muslim Americans in the Los Angeles area. It discusses the impact that the Los Angeles Police Department (LAPD) has had on the perpetration of these hate crimes. Lastly, it proposes a plan for racial bias training for all LAPD officers.
